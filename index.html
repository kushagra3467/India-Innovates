Built for planners and responders

This tool combines historical district-wise rainfall data with a machine learning model of flood occurrence. It is designed to help district administration, disaster management teams, and researchers quickly understand which areas are at higher risk, and why.

Flood early warning Monsoon planning Disaster management Climate resilience

How it works

1

Upload rainfall & elevation data

Start from the curated district rainfall dataset or your own Excel file containing rainfall, elevation, and district information.

2

AI model predicts flood risk

A trained Random Forest model estimates flood probability based on rainfall and elevation, then categorises each row into Low, Medium, or High risk.

3

District-wise insight & suggestions

The app aggregates results by district, showing rainfall statistics, risk levels, and tailored suggestions for before, during, and after flood situations.

Interactive dashboard

The Streamlit dashboard lets you filter by risk level, visualise probability distributions, and download both full predictions and district summaries as CSV files.

  • Filter by Low / Medium / High risk
  • See distribution of flood probability
  • Ward-wise readiness scores (where applicable)
  • District-wise rainfall and risk summary with suggestions
